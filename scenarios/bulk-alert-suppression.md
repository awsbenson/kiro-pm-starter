# Demo Scenario: Bulk Alert Suppression

> Paste this customer email into Kiro to start the class exercises.
> This is used in Modules 1, 3, 4, and 5.

## The Customer Email

```
Hi team,

Our SOC analysts are spending 2 hours every shift dismissing false positive alerts one by one. We have 8 analysts across 2 shifts. They're frustrated and some are talking about switching to CrowdStrike because "at least their tool lets you suppress alerts in bulk."

We need some way to suppress known benign alerts — ideally with rules that auto-expire so we don't accidentally suppress real threats forever. Our CISO wants to see a proposal by end of month.

Thanks,
Sarah (SOC Manager, Customer Alpha — $2.4M ARR, renewal in 38 days)
```

## Key Facts for Exercises

- **Customer:** Alpha, Enterprise tier
- **ARR:** $2.4M
- **Renewal:** 38 days away
- **Competitive threat:** CrowdStrike mentioned by name
- **Pain:** 2 hrs/shift × 8 analysts = 16 analyst-hours/day wasted
- **CISO visibility:** Proposal expected by end of month
- **Cost:** 8 analysts × $75/hr × 2 hrs × 250 days = $300K/year wasted per customer

## How This Scenario Is Used in Each Module

| Module | What Students Do |
