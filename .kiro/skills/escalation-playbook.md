# Escalation Playbook

## Intake Workflow

When receiving a new escalation:

1. **Capture** — Get all raw information (email, Slack, call notes)
2. **Structure** — Separate into: confirmed facts, assumptions, unknowns
3. **Assess** — Determine severity (P0-P3), business impact, stakeholders
4. **Plan** — Create investigation plan with owners and due dates
5. **Communicate** — Draft stakeholder-specific updates
6. **Track** — Create action items with owners, dates, status

## Escalation Spec Template

Every escalation must include:
- Customer name, tier, ARR, renewal date
- Confirmed facts (what we know for certain)
- Assumptions (what we believe but haven't verified)
- Unknowns (what we need to investigate)
- Business impact (revenue, renewal risk, reputation)
- Stakeholder map (who needs what information)
- Investigation plan (each unknown → owner + due date)
- Containment actions (immediate steps)
- Customer communication plan
- Closure criteria (measurable, time-bound)

## Common Mistakes
- Mixing facts with assumptions in customer communications
- Missing owner or due date on action items
- Overpromising fix timelines to customers
- Forgetting to update all stakeholder tracks
- Closing without documented RCA
